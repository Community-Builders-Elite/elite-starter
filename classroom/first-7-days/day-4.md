# Day 4: Get Organized (and Do It for Real)

You can talk to Claude, and you can save your work. Today we make sure everything you create has a clean home, so nothing ever gets lost in a mess.

## Two kinds of "remembering"

It helps to know the difference between two things that sound similar.

Your CLAUDE.md is your business brain. It holds the always-true facts about your business: who you help, what you sell, how you sound. It rarely changes.

Memory is a little different. It's for long-term things you want Claude to remember about how you like to work, even across different days and topics. Things like "always keep my emails short" or "my brand colors are navy and gold." You save a memory just by telling Claude "remember that I always want X."

Here's the rule that keeps it clean: use memory for long-term stuff worth keeping. Do NOT use it for day-to-day things like what you're working on this week. That has its own home, which we'll get to in a second.

## Where your projects live

For the things you're actively working on right now, there's a file called PROJECTS.md. It's a simple list: what's in progress, what's coming up, what's done. When you start something, you tell Claude "add this to my projects," and it keeps the list tidy. When you ask "what am I working on?", Claude reads this first. Simple.

## Your three folders

Your workspace has three main folders, and the idea behind them is dead simple, so nothing gets lost.

Reference is your library. The always-true stuff: your offer, your audience, your voice, your testimonials. Things that don't change much.

Research is your scratchpad. Notes, ideas you're chewing on, things you're looking into. Messy is totally fine here. That's what it's for.

Outputs is your finished work. Anything Claude makes for you that's done: posts, emails, plans. The stuff that's ready to go out into the world.

Each folder also has a file called _master-index.md, which is just a table of contents. Claude keeps it updated so you can always find your stuff. You never have to touch it.

## Today you actually use it

Reading about folders is boring. Using them is what makes it stick. So today you'll do two small real things:

First, you'll save a real note. You tell Claude any idea or thought about your business, even one sentence, and it files it into your research folder and updates the index.

Second, you'll save a real memory. You tell Claude one thing you always want it to remember about how you work, and it saves it.

## Your action in Claude Code

Open your repo, start Claude, and say:

> "Let's do Day 4."

Claude will give you a quick tour of your folders, then have you save a real note and a real memory. Doing it once is all it takes for it to make sense.

## Your win for today

You saved a note AND a memory the right way, and you understand where everything lives. Your workspace is organized, and nothing's going to get lost.
