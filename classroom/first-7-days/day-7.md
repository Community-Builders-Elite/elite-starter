# Day 7: Keep Your Costs Low (and Practice It)

Last day. This one's about money, specifically, how to get a ton of value out of Claude without wasting a cent.

## How cost works, simply

Claude Code costs a little money to run, based on how much work it does. Bigger jobs and longer conversations cost a bit more. For most coaches it's a small monthly cost for a whole lot of help, and you're not going to accidentally spend a fortune. So relax, this isn't about being cheap. It's about not wasting.

## The handful of habits that keep it low

There are only a few habits worth knowing, and they're all easy.

Start fresh for new tasks. When you switch to something totally different, start a new conversation. Long, cluttered chats cost more and actually confuse Claude, because it's carrying all that old stuff around. A clean start is cheaper and gives you sharper answers.

Keep your business brain good. A solid CLAUDE.md means Claude gets things right faster, with less back and forth. Less back and forth is less cost. The work you did on Day 2 is already saving you money.

Be clear about what you want. This is the big one. The clearer your ask, the fewer tries it takes. "Write me a three-sentence Instagram caption about morning routines" gets you a great answer the first time. "Write me something" makes Claude guess, and guessing means do-overs.

Use the right effort for the job. Quick, simple tasks don't need deep thinking. Big, important ones do. You can just tell Claude "keep it quick" or "really think about this one," and it'll match the effort to the task.

## The mindset

Here's the whole thing in one line: the goal isn't to be cheap, it's to not waste. Treat Claude like a smart assistant whose time is worth a little something. Clear asks, fresh starts, a good brain file. That's the game.

## Today you practice it

Reading tips doesn't change behavior. Doing it once does. So today you'll practice the most valuable habit live: giving a clear, specific ask. You'll hand Claude one clear request and see for yourself how a clear ask gets you a better answer the first time, no do-overs. That single habit will save you more than all the others combined.

## Your action in Claude Code

## Your win for today
