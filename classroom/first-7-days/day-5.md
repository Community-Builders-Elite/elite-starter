# Day 5: Build Your Own Skill

This is the day people get hooked. Today you build your own tool, with your own name on it, and you don't write a single line of code.

## What a skill is

A skill is a shortcut. You type a slash command, a word that starts with a slash like /start-here, and Claude does a whole job for you. Instead of explaining what you want every single time, you trigger the skill and it already knows the steps.

Think of skills as buttons for the things you do over and over. Your kit came with a couple already. Today you make your own.

## First, a little helper

There's an official, free helper from the company behind Claude called skill-creator. Its whole job is to make building skills easy. You'll add it with two quick commands (Claude gives you the exact ones in the lesson). If a command name has changed by the time you do this, don't worry, that's not your fault, just tell Claude what you see and it'll sort it out with you.

## The skill you're going to build

You're going to build a call summarizer, called /recap. Here's what it'll do: after a coaching call, you paste in your notes (or a transcript), and it hands you back a clean summary plus a clear list of action items. Every coach does this kind of thing constantly, and Claude is genuinely excellent at it. That's why it's a great first build, it'll actually be useful, and it'll actually work well.

## How you build it (the magic part)

You don't open any code. You just describe what you want in plain English, like "I want a skill called /recap. I paste in my call notes and it gives me a clean summary with action items." Claude builds the skill for you. Then you test it right away by pasting in some notes and typing /recap. It works. That's your win.

## One important thing about good skills

The /recap skill we build today is a simple example, on purpose, so you can see how it works. But here's the real lesson: normally, you want to give a skill as much information and context as you possibly can. Literally step by step, with as much detail as possible, as if you were training a new assistant exactly how you do the task.

The better the prompt, the better the skill. So when you build a real one later, don't be shy. Tell Claude every step of how you'd do it yourself, and the skill will be that much better.

## It repeats for anything

Once you've done it once, you'll realize you can make a skill for almost anything you repeat. You could build a skill that helps you outline a YouTube video, or one that cleans up messy notes, or one that drafts your weekly check-in. Same method every time: describe it in detail, Claude builds it.

## Your action in Claude Code

Open your repo, start Claude, and say:

> "Let's do Day 5."

Claude will add the helper, build your /recap skill, test it with you, and then save it (that commit-and-push habit from Day 3).

## Your win for today

You installed a helper AND built your own working skill. That's a real milestone. Most people never imagine they could build their own tools. You just did.
