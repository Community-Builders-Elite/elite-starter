# Your First 5 Days in Claude Code

A gentle, one-task-a-day plan for your first week. No rush. Do one a day, and by Friday you'll know your way around.

This is the same thing /start-here teaches, just spread out so it never feels like a lot. If you'd rather do it all in one sitting, that's fine too. Just type /start-here and go at your own speed.

How to use this: each day, open your repo in VS Code, start Claude, and tell it which day you're on, like "let's do Day 2." Claude will walk you through it.

---

## Day 1: Tell Claude about your business

**The task:** fill in your CLAUDE.md, your "business brain." It's the file that teaches Claude who you are so it can sound like you.

**Do this:** type /setup-coach and answer the friendly questions, or paste in something you already wrote. Takes about 10 minutes.

**Your win:** ask Claude to write something small, like a quick post or a one line bio, and watch it sound like you. That's your business brain working.

---

## Day 2: Save your work the safe way

**The task:** learn to save and back up your work. It's called committing and pushing, but don't let the words scare you.

**Do this:** just tell Claude "save my work." It takes a snapshot (a commit) and backs it up online to your GitHub (a push). That's it.

**Your win:** your work is now safe online. Even if something happened to your computer, you wouldn't lose it. Make this a habit: any time you finish something, say "save my work."

---

## Day 3: Get organized

**The task:** learn how Claude remembers things, and where your stuff lives.

**Do this:** ask Claude to give you a quick tour of your folders (reference, research, outputs) and your PROJECTS.md file. Then try saving one thing, like "save this idea to my research."

**Your win:** you know where everything goes, so nothing gets lost. Library for always-true facts, scratchpad for notes, outputs for finished work, and a project list for what you're working on.

---

## Day 4: Use a skill

**The task:** learn what skills are. A skill is a shortcut you trigger by typing a slash command, like /start-here.

**Do this:** ask Claude "what skills do I have?" to see your shortcuts. Then, if you want, add a new one by typing /plugin marketplace add anthropics/claude-plugins and then /plugin install skill-creator@claude-plugins-official.

**Your win:** you ran a skill on purpose, and you know how to add more later. Skills are buttons for the things you do over and over.

---

## Day 5: Connect a tool, and keep costs low

**The task:** two quick things. Connect one tool you actually use, and learn the habits that keep your costs low.

**Do this:** tell Claude what tools you use most (email, calendar, notes) and let it walk you through connecting one. A great first pick is Google Workspace. Then ask Claude for its top cost saving tips.

**Your win:** Claude can now do things in a tool you already use, and you know how to get the most out of it without wasting money.

---

## You did it

Five days, and you know the basics. From here, just talk to Claude in plain words and ask it to write, plan, or organize anything.

When you're ready for the next step in your business, that's what the rest of the program is for.
