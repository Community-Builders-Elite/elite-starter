# Classroom Copy: "Your First 5 Days in Claude Code"

Paste-ready copy for the Skool classroom lesson. Everything below the line is the lesson text. Skool supports bold and bullet lists, so the formatting will carry over. Bold the lines marked with ** when you paste, or let Skool's editor handle it.

================================================================

Your first week in Claude Code, made simple.

You just got set up. Now let's build the habit. The plan below gives you one small task a day for five days. By Friday, you'll know your way around and Claude will already be saving you time.

No rush, and nothing here is hard. Each day, open your repo in VS Code, start Claude, and tell it which day you're on, like "let's do Day 2." Claude walks you through the rest.

Want to move faster? You don't have to wait five days. Just type /start-here any time and go at your own speed. This plan is here for anyone who likes one bite at a time.

---

**Day 1: Tell Claude about your business**

Today you fill in your CLAUDE.md, your "business brain." It's the file that teaches Claude who you are, so it can sound like you instead of a generic robot.

Just type /setup-coach and answer the friendly questions, or paste in something you've already written. About 10 minutes.

Your win: ask Claude to write something small, like a quick post or a one line bio, and watch it sound like you.

---

**Day 2: Save your work the safe way**

Today you learn to save and back up your work. The techy words are "commit" and "push," but don't let that scare you.

Just tell Claude "save my work." It takes a snapshot and backs it up online to your GitHub. That's it.

Your win: your work is now safe online. Even if something happened to your computer, you wouldn't lose it. Make it a habit: any time you finish something, say "save my work."

---

**Day 3: Get organized**

Today you learn how Claude remembers things, and where your stuff lives.

Ask Claude to give you a quick tour of your folders (reference, research, outputs) and your projects list. Then try saving one thing, like "save this idea to my research."

Your win: you know where everything goes, so nothing gets lost.

---

**Day 4: Use a skill**

Today you learn what skills are. A skill is a shortcut you trigger by typing a slash command, like /start-here.

Ask Claude "what skills do I have?" to see your shortcuts. Want to add a new one? Type /plugin marketplace add anthropics/claude-plugins, then /plugin install skill-creator@claude-plugins-official.

Your win: you ran a skill on purpose, and you know how to add more later.

---

**Day 5: Connect a tool, and keep costs low**

Two quick things today. Connect one tool you actually use, and learn the habits that keep your costs low.

Tell Claude what tools you use most (email, calendar, notes) and let it walk you through connecting one. A great first pick is Google Workspace. Then ask Claude for its top cost saving tips.

Your win: Claude can now do things in a tool you already use, and you know how to get the most out of it without wasting money.

---

That's it. Five days, and you've got the basics down.

From here, just talk to Claude in plain words and ask it to write, plan, or organize anything you need. When you're ready for the next step in your business, that's what the rest of the program is for.
