# Your First 7 Days in Claude Code

A gentle, one-task-a-day plan for your first week. Each day you actually DO one thing, so by the end of the week you've built real skills, not just watched.

This is the same thing /start-here teaches, just spread out so it never feels like a lot. Prefer to go faster? Type /start-here any time and go at your own speed.

How to use this: each day, open your repo in VS Code, start Claude, and tell it which day you're on, like "let's do Day 2." Claude walks you through it and has you do the action yourself.

---

## Day 1: Make it yours

**What you do:** make sure you're working in your own copy of this kit. Most likely you already are, so this is a quick check. If not, Claude helps you get your own copy.

**Your win:** you've got your own workspace, and the techy setup is behind you.

---

## Day 2: Tell Claude about your business

**What you do:** fill in your CLAUDE.md, your "business brain." Type /setup-coach and answer the friendly questions, or paste in something you already wrote. About 10 minutes.

**Your win:** ask Claude to write something small, like a quick bio, and watch it sound like you.

---

## Day 3: Save your work the safe way

**What you do:** learn to save and back up your work. Just tell Claude "save my work." It takes a snapshot and backs it up online to your GitHub.

**Your win:** your work is safe online. Make it a habit: any time you finish something, say "save my work."

---

## Day 4: Get organized (and do it for real)

**What you do:** learn how Claude remembers things and where your files live. Then actually use it: save one real note to your research folder, and save one long-term memory, like "always keep my emails short."

**Your win:** you saved a note AND a memory the right way. Nothing gets lost.

---

## Day 5: Build your own skill

**What you do:** the big one. You install a free helper called skill-creator, then you build your very own skill, a call summarizer called /recap. You don't write any code, you just describe what you want and Claude builds it. Then you test it on real call notes.

A quick heads up: the one you build this day is a simple example. Normally you want to give a skill as much detail as you can, step by step. The better you describe it, the better the skill.

**Your win:** you built a working tool with your own name on it.

---

## Day 6: Connect a tool you use

**What you do:** connect one tool you already use so Claude can work in it. Tell Claude what you use most (email, calendar, notes) and let it walk you through connecting one. A great first pick is Google Workspace.

**Your win:** Claude can now do things in a tool you already use, like read your calendar.

---

## Day 7: Keep your costs low (and practice it)

**What you do:** learn the habits that keep your costs low, then practice the biggest one live: give Claude one clear, specific ask and see how a clear ask gets a better answer the first time.

**Your win:** you know how to get the most out of Claude without wasting money.

---

## You did it

Seven days, and you've got the basics down. You filled in your brain, saved your work, got organized, built your own skill, connected a tool, and learned to keep costs low.

From here, just talk to Claude in plain words and ask it to write, plan, or organize anything. When you're ready for the next step in your business, that's what the rest of the program is for.
