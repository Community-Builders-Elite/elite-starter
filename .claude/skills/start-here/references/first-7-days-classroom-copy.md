# Classroom Copy: "Your First 7 Days in Claude Code"

Paste-ready copy for the Skool classroom lesson. Everything below the line is the lesson text. Skool supports bold and bullet lists. Bold the lines marked with ** when you paste, or let Skool's editor handle it.

================================================================

Your first week in Claude Code, made simple.

You just got set up. Now let's build the habit. The plan below gives you one small task a day for seven days, and each day you actually DO something. By the end of the week, you'll have built real skills, not just watched videos about them.

No rush, and nothing here is hard. Each day, open your repo in VS Code, start Claude, and tell it which day you're on, like "let's do Day 2." Claude walks you through it.

Want to move faster? You don't have to wait. Just type /start-here any time and go at your own speed. This plan is here for anyone who likes one bite at a time.

---

**Day 1: Make it yours**

Make sure you're working in your own copy of this kit. You probably already are, so this is a quick check. If not, Claude helps you get your own copy.

Your win: your own workspace is ready, and the techy setup is behind you.

---

**Day 2: Tell Claude about your business**

Fill in your CLAUDE.md, your "business brain." It teaches Claude who you are so it can sound like you. Type /setup-coach and answer the friendly questions, or paste in something you've already written. About 10 minutes.

Your win: ask Claude to write something small, like a quick bio, and watch it sound like you.

---

**Day 3: Save your work the safe way**

Learn to save and back up your work. The techy words are "commit" and "push," but don't let that scare you. Just tell Claude "save my work." It takes a snapshot and backs it up online to your GitHub.

Your win: your work is safe online. Make it a habit: any time you finish something, say "save my work."

---

**Day 4: Get organized (and do it for real)**

Learn how Claude remembers things and where your files live. Then actually use it: save one real note to your research folder, and save one long-term memory, like "always keep my emails short."

Your win: you saved a note AND a memory the right way, so nothing gets lost.

---

**Day 5: Build your own skill**

The big one. You install a free helper called skill-creator, then you build your very own skill, a call summarizer called /recap. You don't write any code. You just describe what you want and Claude builds it for you. Then you test it on real call notes.

Quick heads up: the one you build this day is a simple example. Normally you want to give a skill as much detail as you can, step by step. The better you describe it, the better the skill.

Your win: you built a working tool with your own name on it.

---

**Day 6: Connect a tool you use**

Connect one tool you already use so Claude can work in it, not just talk about it. Tell Claude what you use most (email, calendar, notes) and let it walk you through connecting one. A great first pick is Google Workspace.

Your win: Claude can now do things in a tool you already use, like read your calendar.

---

**Day 7: Keep your costs low (and practice it)**

Learn the habits that keep your costs low, then practice the biggest one live. Give Claude one clear, specific ask and see how a clear ask gets you a better answer the first time.

Your win: you know how to get the most out of Claude without wasting money.

---

That's it. Seven days, and you've got the basics down.

You filled in your brain, saved your work, got organized, built your own skill, connected a tool, and learned to keep costs low. From here, just talk to Claude in plain words and ask it to write, plan, or organize anything. When you're ready for the next step in your business, that's what the rest of the program is for.
